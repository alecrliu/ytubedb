We used YouTube Data API v3 for our website data. YouTube API has a daily limit of 10k units. Retrieving information of either a channel, video, or playlist or getting playlists of a channel (max 50 results per page) costs 1 unit. And getting videos of a channel (max 50 results per page) costs 100 units. Thus, we had to optimize API calls to extract maximum information without exceeding the limit.

Our strategy was to get various channel IDs manually and then make a streamlined process of collecting all information related to that channel ID (including playlists and videos) and storing it in separate JSON files: channelsJSON for channel information, playlistsJSON for playlist and associated videos information, and videosJSON for stray videos of channels. The process is as follows:

  1. Load the current JSON files.
  2. Check if a channel ID is not present in the channelsJSON.
  3. Get channel information using channel ID.
  4. Get at most 10 playlists of that channel using channel ID.
  5. While getting the playlists, remove all videos from the playlists that aren't from that channel. If all videos of the playlists are from different channels then that playlist is discarded.
  6. Get another batch of at most 25 videos from that channel if the videos of playlists don't sum up more than 25.
  7. Store all the information in the JSON files.
